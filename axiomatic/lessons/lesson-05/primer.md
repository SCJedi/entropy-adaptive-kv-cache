# Primer: The Cell Vacuum -- A Different Ground State

## The Problem Recap

The mode vacuum |0⟩ has infinite energy density. When you try to make it more precise (refine the cutoff), the energy density gets worse, not better. This violates the refinability axiom (A1). We need a different vacuum.

## The Big Idea

Every massive particle has a natural length scale called the **Compton wavelength**:

$$\lambda_C = \frac{\hbar}{mc}$$

Below this scale, you can't localize the particle without creating particle-antiparticle pairs. For a neutrino with mass ~2.3 meV, λ_C ≈ 85 micrometers.

Idea: divide space into cells of size λ_C. Put one quantum harmonic oscillator in each cell. Make each oscillator a coherent state with energy mc². This is the **cell vacuum** |Ω⟩.

## What You Get

**Finite energy density:**
$$\rho_{\text{cell}} = \frac{m^4c^5}{\hbar^3} \approx 6 \times 10^{-10} \text{ J/m}^3$$

This is the only combination of m, c, ℏ with dimensions of energy density. It matches the observed dark energy density exactly (for neutrino mass m ≈ 2.3 meV).

**Zero entanglement:** The cell vacuum is a product state -- no entanglement across any boundary. The mode vacuum has enormous boundary entanglement (~Area).

**Equation of state w = 0:** From the virial theorem, kinetic and potential energy balance, so pressure is zero. The cell vacuum behaves like dust, not like dark energy (w = -1).

**Broken Lorentz invariance:** The cell structure picks a preferred frame. This is the cost of finiteness.

## The Tradeoff

You can't have both finiteness and Lorentz invariance. The mode vacuum keeps Lorentz invariance but has infinite energy. The cell vacuum has finite energy but breaks Lorentz invariance.

Which matters more? We argue: finiteness is mandatory (axiom F). Lorentz invariance is beautiful but empirically broken by cosmology anyway (the CMB defines a preferred frame).

## Is This Legal?

Yes. Algebraic quantum field theory proves that inequivalent representations of the field algebra exist (Haag's theorem, GNS construction). The mode vacuum |0⟩ and cell vacuum |Ω⟩ are both valid representations. The axioms A0--F select which one is physical.

## Axiom Scorecard

Cell vacuum: passes all 7 axioms (A0, A1, P, Q, M', L, F).

Mode vacuum: fails 2 axioms (A1 refinability, F finiteness).
