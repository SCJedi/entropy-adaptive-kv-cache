# Primer: The Selection Principle

## The Big Idea

We've audited two vacuum constructions against seven axioms. The result: only the cell vacuum passes all seven. This is a **selection theorem** — consistency uniquely determines the vacuum. It's not a choice or convention; it's a mathematical consequence of demanding that the vacuum satisfy the axioms.

The physical implication: the cell vacuum has w = 0 (cold dark matter), predicts neutrino masses Σmν ≈ 60 meV, and gives finite energy density without regularization. Dark energy (w = -1) remains unexplained — it's geometry (Λ), not quantum field theory.

## The Selection Theorem

**Setup:** Two vacuum candidates:
- Mode vacuum |0⟩: standard QFT vacuum, Lorentz invariant, infinite energy
- Cell vacuum |Ω⟩: product of coherent states, preferred frame, finite energy

**Test:** Run each through seven axioms (A0, A1, P, Q, M', L, F)

**Result:**
- Mode vacuum: fails A1 (refinability) and F (finiteness)
- Cell vacuum: passes all seven

**Conclusion:** Among {mode, cell}, only the cell vacuum is consistent with the axiom system. [PROVEN]

This is not "we prefer this vacuum" — it's "this is the only consistent vacuum among those tested."

## Why This Matters

In standard QFT, the vacuum is "the state with lowest energy." But this definition fails when:
1. Energy is infinite (mode vacuum: ⟨0|H|0⟩ = ∞)
2. Spacetime is curved (no unique vacuum)
3. Multiple representations exist (unitarily inequivalent vacua)

Axiomatic selection replaces "lowest energy" with "satisfies consistency conditions." This is like how axiomatic set theory (ZF axioms) resolves Russell's paradox by ruling out inconsistent constructions.

## The Physical Consequence: w = 0

The cell vacuum has equation of state w = p/ρ = 0. This means:
- w = 0: cold dark matter (pressureless, non-relativistic)
- w = -1: dark energy (cosmological constant)
- w = 1/3: radiation (relativistic)

The cell vacuum is **cold dark matter**, not dark energy.

In an expanding universe, energy density evolves as ρ(a) ∝ a^(-3(1+w)):
- w = 0 → ρ ∝ a^(-3) (dilutes like matter)
- w = -1 → ρ = constant (cosmological constant)

Observations confirm dark energy has w ≈ -1, while dark matter has w ≈ 0. The cell vacuum matches dark matter, not dark energy.

## The Neutrino Mass Prediction

If ρ_cell = ρ_CDM (observed cold dark matter density), we can solve for the mass:

ρ_cell = m⁴c⁵/ℏ³ = 4.4×10⁻¹⁰ J/m³

gives:

m = 1.77 meV

If this is the lightest neutrino mass m₁, the full spectrum (using oscillation data) is:
- m₁ = 1.77 meV
- m₂ = 9.0 meV
- m₃ = 49.6 meV
- Sum: Σmν ≈ 60.4 meV

Current bound (DESI 2024): Σmν < 53 meV (95% CL). Mild tension (~1.5σ). Future experiments (CMB-S4) will have σ(Σmν) ~ 15 meV, allowing a definitive test.

This is a **testable prediction**. If confirmed, strong evidence for the cell vacuum. If ruled out, the framework is falsified.

## What About Dark Energy?

The cell vacuum does NOT explain dark energy. It has:
- ρ_cell ≈ 5.94×10⁻¹⁰ J/m³ (matches dark matter density)
- w = 0 (wrong — dark energy has w ≈ -1)

Dark energy is the **cosmological constant Λ**, a geometric term in Einstein's equation:

G_μν + Λg_μν = 8πG⟨T_μν⟩

Λ is on the geometry side, not the matter side. It describes spacetime curvature, not quantum field stress-energy.

The cell vacuum addresses the quantum vacuum contribution (cold dark matter, w = 0). The cosmological constant problem (why is Λ small but nonzero?) remains open.

## Comparison: Renormalization vs Selection

**Renormalization (standard approach):**
- Keep mode vacuum |0⟩
- Subtract infinity: :H: = H - ⟨0|H|0⟩
- Add Λ by hand to match observations
- Pros: Lorentz invariant, tested in particle physics
- Cons: subtraction is ad hoc, doesn't predict Λ

**Axiomatic Selection (this framework):**
- Replace mode vacuum with cell vacuum |Ω⟩
- Energy is finite: ⟨Ω|H|Ω⟩ = m⁴c⁵/ℏ³
- Λ is separate (geometry)
- Pros: no infinities, predicts vacuum energy density
- Cons: not Lorentz invariant, physical realization uncertain

Which is correct? Empirical question. The neutrino mass sum Σmν ≈ 60 meV is a discriminator.
