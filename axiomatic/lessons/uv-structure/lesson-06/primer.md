# Primer: UV Structure and the Alpha Framework

## The Framework's Choice

The Alpha Framework accepts axioms A1 (refinability) and F (finiteness). As shown in Lesson 5, this is equivalent to accepting a natural UV scale.

The framework identifies this scale as the Compton wavelength:

$$\lambda_{UV} = \lambda_C = \frac{\hbar}{mc}$$

Why Compton? Because it's where pair creation prevents further localization. Below $\lambda_C$, you're creating particles, not probing the vacuum. It's a physical threshold, not an arbitrary choice.

## The Cell Vacuum

With this UV scale, the vacuum energy is:

$$\rho = \frac{m^4 c^5}{\hbar^3}$$

This is dimensionally unique -- the only energy density you can build from $m$, $c$, $\hbar$.

For the lightest neutrino ($m \approx 1.77$ meV):
$$\rho \approx 5.9 \times 10^{-10} \text{ J/m}^3$$

This matches the observed cold dark matter density to within a factor of 1.3.

## The Equation of State

The virial theorem gives $w = p/\rho = 0$.

**This is dark matter, not dark energy.**

Dark energy has $w = -1$. The cell vacuum does NOT explain dark energy -- that remains the cosmological constant $\Lambda$ in Einstein's equation.

## What's Explained, What's Not

**Explained:**
- Why renormalization works (probes scales $\ll \lambda_C$)
- Why the "CC problem" isn't really $10^{123}$ (wrong vacuum assumption)
- The dark matter density (roughly)

**Not explained:**
- Why $\Lambda \neq 0$ (dark energy)
- Why the neutrino mass is what it is

The framework is honest: this is a choice with testable consequences, not a derivation from first principles.
