# Primer: The UV Choice and Its Consequences

## The Fundamental Choice

There are two mutually exclusive options for UV structure:

**Option A: No natural UV scale**
- Sum over all modes to infinity
- Integrals diverge
- Exact Lorentz invariance
- Only differences are meaningful

**Option B: Natural UV scale**
- Sum terminates at physical scale
- Integrals are finite
- Lorentz invariance broken at small scales
- Absolute values are meaningful

These aren't approximations to the same thing. They're different theories.

## The Equivalence Theorem

The key insight of this lesson is a proven equivalence:

$$\text{(No UV scale)} \Leftrightarrow \text{(A1 fails)} \Leftrightarrow \text{(F fails)} \Leftrightarrow \text{(Exact Lorentz)}$$

Where:
- **A1** = Refinability (state converges as you describe it more finely)
- **F** = Finiteness (observables have finite values)

If you want exact Lorentz invariance, you get infinite vacuum energy. If you want finite vacuum energy, you break Lorentz at small scales. There's no escape.

## The Two Packages

**Package 1 (Standard QFT):**
- Exact Lorentz invariance
- A1 fails, F fails
- Renormalization required
- Vacuum energy undefined
- Mode vacuum is the ground state

**Package 2 (Cell vacuum):**
- Lorentz broken at UV scale, emergent at large scales
- A1 satisfied, F satisfied
- No renormalization needed for vacuum energy
- $\rho = m^4c^5/\hbar^3$
- Cell vacuum is the ground state

## Why Compton?

If you choose Package 2, the Compton wavelength is the natural UV scale:
- It's where pair creation kicks in
- It's determined by physics, not imposed
- For the lightest neutrino, $\lambda_C \sim 0.1$ mm
- The resulting $\rho \sim 10^{-10}$ J/m$^3$ matches dark matter density

The choice between packages is fundamental. The Alpha Framework chooses Package 2.
