# F-07: What Is Energy?

Welcome! You're about to learn one of the most important concepts in all of science: energy. By the end of this lesson, you'll understand what energy really means and why it's so important. You can do this.

## What Is Energy?

Energy is the ability to make things happen.

That's it. That's the core idea.

When something has energy, it can:
- Move other things
- Heat things up
- Make light
- Make sounds
- Change things

When something has NO energy, it can't do anything. It just sits there.

## Energy You Can See

Let's look at examples of energy you experience every day:

**A moving ball:**
```
     →→→
    ●●●●  ← Ball rolling
```

The ball has energy because it's moving. If it hits a pin, the pin falls over. The ball made something happen - that's energy in action.

**A stretched rubber band:**
```
Normal:  ======
Stretched: ============
```

When you stretch a rubber band, you give it energy. Let go, and it snaps back - it makes something happen (it moves).

**Hot soup:**
```
  ~~~~  ← steam rising
 (●●●●)
 [SOUP]  ← hot!
```

The soup has energy because it's hot. It can warm your hands, make steam rise, or burn your tongue. Heat IS a form of energy.

## Different Forms of Energy

Energy comes in different forms, but it's all the same basic thing: the ability to make things happen.

**Kinetic Energy (Energy of Motion):**

Anything moving has kinetic energy:
- A thrown baseball
- A flowing river
- Wind blowing
- Your hand waving

The faster something moves, the more kinetic energy it has.

```
Slow ball:  →     Small kinetic energy
Fast ball:  →→→→  Large kinetic energy
```

**Potential Energy (Stored Energy):**

Energy can be stored, ready to be released:

A book on a high shelf:
```
       [BOOK]  ← High up = stored energy
         |
         | (if it falls, energy released)
         |
         ↓
     [FLOOR]
```

When the book falls, the stored energy becomes kinetic energy (motion).

A compressed spring:
```
Normal spring:  ====
Compressed:     ==  ← Stored energy!
```

When released, the spring pushes outward - the stored energy becomes motion.

**Thermal Energy (Heat):**

Hot things have energy in the random motion of their tiny particles:

```
Cold water: ●  ●  ●    (particles moving slowly)
            ●  ●  ●

Hot water:  ●→ ●← ●↑   (particles moving fast!)
            ●↓ ●→ ●←
```

The hotter something is, the more thermal energy it has.

**Chemical Energy:**

Energy stored in substances that can be released:

Food:
```
[BANANA] → (you eat it) → Energy for your body
```

Batteries:
```
[BATTERY] → (powers device) → Electricity
```

Gasoline:
```
[FUEL] → (burns in engine) → Car moves
```

## The Most Important Rule: Conservation of Energy

Here's the big secret about energy:

**Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only change forms.**

This is called the Law of Conservation of Energy, and it's one of the most important laws in all of science.

Let's see it in action:

**Dropping a ball:**
```
High up:     [BALL]     ← Has potential energy (stored)
              |
    Falling:  ● ↓       ← Energy becoming kinetic (motion)
              |
    Ground:   ●         ← Energy becomes heat and sound
            [GROUND]
```

Energy flow:
1. Potential energy (ball is high)
2. → Kinetic energy (ball is falling)
3. → Heat and sound energy (ball hits ground)

The energy never disappeared. It just changed forms.

**Charging your phone:**
```
Wall outlet → Charger → Battery → Phone screen lighting up
(electrical)  (electrical) (chemical) (light + heat)
```

The energy transformed several times, but the total amount stayed the same.

## Energy Transfer

Energy can move from one thing to another:

**You throw a ball:**
```
Your arm → Ball
(chemical energy in muscles) → (kinetic energy of ball)
```

**Sunlight warms a rock:**
```
Sun → Rock
(light energy) → (heat energy in rock)
```

**Wind turns a windmill:**
```
Wind → Windmill blades → Electricity
(kinetic) → (kinetic) → (electrical)
```

Energy flows from place to place, changing forms as it goes.

## Measuring Energy

We measure energy in units called joules (J).

For everyday examples:
- Lifting an apple 1 meter: about 1 joule
- A candy bar: about 1 million joules (1 megajoule)
- Driving a car 1 mile: about 2 million joules

You don't need to memorize these numbers. Just know that energy can be measured, like distance or weight.

## Where Does Energy Come From?

If energy can't be created, where did all the energy in the universe come from?

**Short answer:** It was there from the beginning. The Big Bang created all the energy in the universe about 13.8 billion years ago, and it's been changing forms ever since.

**Practical answer:** For everyday purposes, energy comes from:
- The Sun (light and heat)
- Food (chemical energy from plants and animals)
- Fuel (coal, oil, gas - ancient stored sunlight)
- Batteries (stored chemical energy)

All of these trace back to the Sun or to energy stored long ago.

## Why Energy Matters

Energy is important because:

**Nothing happens without it:**

Want to move? You need energy.
Want to think? Your brain needs energy.
Want to heat your house? You need energy.

**It's conserved:**

You can track where energy goes. If you "lose" energy, it didn't disappear - it changed forms (usually into heat that spreads out).

**It limits what's possible:**

You can't get something for nothing. If you want to lift a heavy box, you need to supply energy. The universe keeps perfect accounting.

## Energy Transformations in Your Life

Let's trace energy in something you do every day: riding a bike.

```
1. You eat breakfast → Chemical energy stored in your body
2. You pedal → Chemical energy → Kinetic energy of legs
3. Legs turn pedals → Kinetic energy → Kinetic energy of bike
4. Bike moves → Kinetic energy → You go forward!
5. Friction with ground → Some energy → Heat

Energy flow: Food → Your muscles → Bike motion → Heat (from friction)
```

Every action you take involves energy changing forms and flowing from one thing to another.

## Answers to Exercises

1. a) Kinetic energy (energy of motion)
   b) Chemical energy (stored in the battery)
   c) Thermal energy (heat)

2. a) FALSE - energy cannot be created (conservation law)
   b) TRUE - anything moving has kinetic energy
   c) TRUE - energy changes forms all the time

3. Energy flow:
   - Chemical energy in the battery
   - → Electrical energy in the circuit
   - → Light energy from the bulb
   - → Heat energy (bulbs always waste some energy as heat)

4. It depends! Kinetic energy depends on BOTH mass and speed. The slow truck might have more energy (because it's heavy) even though the bicycle is faster. Or the bicycle might have more if it's going really fast and the truck is barely moving. We need actual numbers to compare.

5. The kinetic energy becomes:
   - Sound energy (you hear a thud)
   - Heat energy (the book and floor get very slightly warmer from the impact)
   - Some energy might cause a tiny deformation (bend) in the floor or book

   All the energy is still there - just spread out in forms that are hard to gather back together.
