## What It's Good For

**The capability:** The cell vacuum gives finite, absolute values for quantities that QFT leaves undefined.

| Question | QFT Answer | Cell Vacuum Answer |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| What is the vacuum energy density? | ∞ or "undefined" | ρ = m⁴c⁵/ℏ³ ≈ 6×10⁻¹⁰ J/m³ |

**When you need this:** Any time you need to plug vacuum properties into another equation — especially Einstein's equations for gravity.

### 2. Coupling Quantum Fields to Gravity

**The problem:** Einstein's equations need T_μν. QFT gives you ∞.

**The solution:** Cell vacuum gives you a finite T_μν:

$$T_{\mu\nu} = \rho \, u_\mu u_\nu$$

where ρ = m⁴c⁵/ℏ³ and u_μ is the 4-velocity of the cell frame.

**What this enables:**
- Semiclassical gravity with a well-defined vacuum
- Cosmological calculations without ∞ - ∞ subtractions
- A finite source term for dark matter

### 4. Explaining Why Renormalization Works

**The puzzle:** Why does subtracting ∞ from ∞ give correct answers?

**The framework's answer:** Because particle physics only asks F-weak questions (differences). The mode vacuum is fine for differences. The infinity only matters for absolute questions, which particle physics doesn't ask.

**What this clarifies:**
- Renormalization isn't a trick — it's the right tool for relative questions
- The infinity isn't "swept under the rug" — it's in a sector particle physics doesn't probe
- QFT isn't broken — it's being used correctly for its domain

## What It's NOT Good For

### 1. Particle Physics Calculations

**Don't use it for:** Scattering amplitudes, decay rates, cross-sections, Feynman diagrams.

**Why not:** The mode vacuum + renormalization is the right tool. It's been tested to 12 decimal places. Cell vacuum gives the same answers with more work.

**Use instead:** Standard QFT.

---

### 2. Explaining Dark Energy

**Don't use it for:** Deriving why Λ ≠ 0 or predicting the dark energy density.

**Why not:** Cell vacuum has w = 0, not w = -1. It's dark matter, not dark energy. Λ remains a free parameter.

**The honest status:** Dark energy is unexplained. The framework says it must be geometric (Λ), not a quantum vacuum effect. But it doesn't say what Λ is.

---

### 3. Quantum Gravity

**Don't use it for:** Black hole information paradox, Planck-scale physics, unification.

**Why not:** The framework is semiclassical — quantum fields on classical spacetime. It doesn't quantize gravity.

**What it does say:** The cell vacuum has zero entropy (tension with Bekenstein-Hawking). This is an open problem, not a solution.

---

### 4. High-Precision Lorentz Tests

**Don't use it for:** Predicting Lorentz violation at colliders.

**Why not:** The Lorentz breaking is at the Compton scale (~0.1 mm for neutrinos). This is MUCH larger than collider scales. No observable Lorentz violation is predicted at LHC.

---

### 5. Replacing Standard QFT

**Don't use it for:** A wholesale replacement of quantum field theory.

**Why not:** Standard QFT works. The cell vacuum is a different vacuum STATE, not a different theory. The dynamics (Lagrangian, propagators, interactions) are unchanged.
