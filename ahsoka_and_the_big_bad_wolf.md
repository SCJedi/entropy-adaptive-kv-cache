# Ahsoka and the Big Bad Wolf

## A Galaxy Far, Far Away Fairy Tale

Once upon a time, in the Outer Rim of the galaxy, there lived three little Jawas on the dusty moon of Arvala. Their names were Teek, Meek, and Zeek. They had grown tired of living in their clan's sandcrawler and decided to build homes of their own near a small trading outpost.

Teek, the youngest, built his home out of scrap metal panels he found in a junkyard. It was quick work, and he spent the rest of his day collecting droids to sell.

Meek, the middle brother, built his home from plasteel crates stacked together. It took a bit longer, but he was still finished by midday and went off to trade with passing travelers.

Zeek, the eldest and wisest, took his time. He welded together thick durasteel plates, reinforced the walls, and installed a proper security system. It took him many rotations, but when he finished, his home was a small fortress.

Now, in this part of the galaxy, there prowled a fearsome creature known as the Big Bad Loth Wolf. He was enormous, with fur as dark as the void of space and eyes that glowed like dying stars. He had been corrupted by the dark side of the Force, and he used his powers to terrorize settlements and take whatever he wanted.

One evening, the Big Bad Loth Wolf came upon Teeks little scrap metal shack. He could smell the valuable kyber crystals the Jawa had collected inside.

Little Jawa, little Jawa, let me in, the wolf growled, his voice rumbling like a starship engine.

Utinni! No way! squeaked Teek from inside.

The wolf smiled a terrible smile. Then I will huff, and I will puff, and I will use the Force to blow your house in.

The wolf closed his eyes, drew upon the dark side, and with a mighty push of Force energy, he sent the scrap metal walls flying in all directions. Teek barely escaped, scrambling across the sand toward his brothers home.

The wolf pursued him to Meeks plasteel crate house. Now there were two little Jawas hiding inside.

Little Jawas, little Jawas, let me in, the wolf demanded.

Utinni! Never! they cried together.

Then I will huff, and I will puff, and I will use the Force to blow your house in.

Again the wolf gathered the dark side around him. With an even mightier blast, the plasteel crates exploded outward, scattering across the dunes. The two brothers fled toward Zeeks fortress home.

The wolf followed, confident in his power. He stood before the durasteel structure and called out once more.

Little Jawas, little Jawas, let me in.

From inside, three small voices shouted together. Utinni! You cannot have us!

The wolf laughed. Then I will huff, and I will puff, and I will use the Force to blow your house in.

He reached deep into the dark side, deeper than ever before. He pushed with all his might. The durasteel walls groaned and shuddered, but they held firm. He tried again and again, but Zeeks craftsmanship would not yield.

Frustrated and furious, the wolf decided to climb onto the roof and enter through the ventilation shaft. He leaped up with Force enhanced agility and began to squeeze his massive body into the opening.

But the Jawas had sent out a distress signal, and someone had answered.

A ship descended from the sky, its engines cutting through the twilight. From the landing ramp emerged a Togruta woman with orange skin and white facial markings. Two white lightsabers hummed to life in her hands, casting a silver glow across the sand.

Her name was Ahsoka Tano.

Hey, she called out calmly to the wolf, who was now stuck halfway in the ventilation shaft. I think you picked the wrong house tonight.

The Big Bad Loth Wolf snarled and pulled himself free, dropping to the ground to face her. He bared his fangs and gathered the dark side around him like a cloak.

You dare challenge me, Jedi? he growled.

Ahsoka smiled slightly. I am no Jedi. But I am someone who protects those who cannot protect themselves.

The wolf lunged at her with Force enhanced speed, but Ahsoka was faster. She had trained under Anakin Skywalker himself and had survived Order Sixty Six, the Siege of Mandalore, and encounters with Darth Vader. A corrupted Loth Wolf, fearsome as he was, found himself outmatched.

They battled across the moonlit dunes. The wolf slashed with claws and snapped with jaws, hurling debris with the Force. Ahsoka flowed like water around every attack, her twin blades weaving a defensive pattern that seemed almost effortless.

She did not seek to destroy him. She sensed that beneath the darkness, there was still a creature of the light, one of the mystical wolves connected to the Force itself, twisted by some ancient Sith corruption.

As the wolf tired, Ahsoka reached out, not with her weapons but with the Force itself. She touched the light that still flickered within him, buried deep beneath layers of darkness and pain.

You were not always this way, she said softly, deactivating her lightsabers. The dark side has used you, but it does not own you. You can choose another path.

The wolf hesitated. Something stirred within him, a memory of running through tall grass on Lothal, of guiding lost travelers, of being a guardian rather than a destroyer.

With a great shudder, the darkness began to lift. The wolves eyes, once burning with malevolent fire, softened to a deep and gentle gold. He shrank slightly, his fur lightening from void black to a noble gray.

He lowered his great head before Ahsoka.

I remember now, he said, his voice no longer a growl but something ancient and wise. I was a protector once, long ago. Thank you for reminding me.

Ahsoka placed a hand on his broad forehead. We all lose our way sometimes. What matters is finding the path back.

The three Jawa brothers emerged cautiously from their fortress home. When they saw the transformed wolf, they chattered excitedly among themselves.

The wolf turned to them and bowed his head. Little ones, I am sorry for the fear I caused and the homes I destroyed. I will help you rebuild, if you will allow it.

The Jawas looked at each other, then at Ahsoka, who nodded encouragingly. Finally, Zeek stepped forward.

Utinni, he said, which in this case meant, We accept.

And so the Big Bad Loth Wolf became the Guardian of Arvala. He helped the Jawas build new homes, stronger than before, and he watched over the trading outpost, protecting travelers from pirates and predators alike.

Ahsoka stayed for a while, sharing stories with the Jawas and teaching the wolf to meditate, to keep the darkness at bay. When it came time for her to leave, the wolf walked beside her to her ship.

Will you return? he asked.

Ahsoka looked up at the stars. The galaxy is large, and there is much work to do. But I have a feeling our paths will cross again.

She climbed the ramp and paused at the top. May the Force be with you, Guardian.

And with you, Ahsoka Tano.

Her ship rose into the sky, became a point of light, and then vanished into hyperspace.

The wolf sat watching for a long time, then turned and padded back toward the outpost, where three little Jawas were waiting with a warm meal and warmer company.

And they all lived peacefully, for many rotations to come.

The End.
