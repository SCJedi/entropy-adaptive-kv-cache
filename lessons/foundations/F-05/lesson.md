# F-05: Patterns and Sequences

**Prerequisites:** F-01 through F-04 (counting, fractions, multiplication, negative numbers)

---

## The World is Full of Patterns

Look around your kitchen. The tiles on the floor repeat. The ticking of a clock repeats. The seasons repeat. Your heartbeat repeats. Nature loves patterns.

And once you spot a pattern, you can predict what comes next. That's incredibly powerful.

---

## Simple Number Patterns

Here's a pattern:

```
1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...
```

What comes next? Obviously 6. The rule is "add one each time."

Here's another:

```
2, 4, 6, 8, 10, ...
```

What comes next? 12. The rule is "add two each time" (or "count by twos").

The dots `...` mean "and this pattern continues forever."

---

## Growing Patterns

A pattern that gets bigger is a **growing pattern**.

```
5, 10, 15, 20, 25, ...
```

Each number is 5 bigger than the one before. This is counting by fives.

Why does this matter? Imagine you're stacking boxes. Each box is 5 inches tall. How tall is a stack of 4 boxes? You're counting: 5, 10, 15, 20 inches. The pattern tells you the height.

Here's a faster-growing pattern:

```
1, 2, 4, 8, 16, ...
```

What's happening here? Each number is **double** the previous one. This pattern grows quickly. By the 10th number, you'd be at 1,024.

This pattern shows up everywhere: bacteria doubling, compound interest, spreading rumors. Understanding fast-growing patterns helps you see when things might get out of control fast.

---

## Shrinking Patterns

Patterns can also get smaller.

```
20, 15, 10, 5, 0, -5, ...
```

Each number drops by 5. Remember negative numbers from F-04? When a pattern keeps shrinking, it can go right through zero into the negatives.

Here's another shrinking pattern:

```
100, 50, 25, 12.5, 6.25, ...
```

Each number is **half** the previous one. This shrinks fast but never quite reaches zero. Think of a ball bouncing: each bounce is half the height of the previous one. It gets smaller and smaller, but never completely stops.

---

## Patterns With a Twist

Not all patterns just add or multiply.

```
1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ...
```

What's the rule? Look closer:

- 1 = 1 × 1
- 4 = 2 × 2
- 9 = 3 × 3
- 16 = 4 × 4
- 25 = 5 × 5

These are the **square numbers**. Each number is multiplied by itself. The next would be 6 × 6 = 36.

Why "square"? Because if you arrange 16 dots in a square, you get 4 rows of 4. It makes a square shape.

---

## Why Patterns Matter in Physics

Everything in the universe follows patterns:

- **Pendulums swing** in a repeating pattern
- **Planets orbit** in repeating patterns
- **Waves on water** repeat their up-and-down motion
- **Light and sound** are repeating patterns moving through space

Once you learn to recognize patterns, you can predict how the world behaves. That's what physics is about: finding the patterns nature uses.

A wave is just a pattern that moves. We'll get there soon, but first you need to be comfortable with patterns themselves.

---

## Finding the Rule

When you see a pattern, your job is to figure out the rule:

- **What's changing?** (getting bigger? smaller?)
- **By how much?** (adding 3? doubling?)
- **Is it steady?** (same change each time? or accelerating?)

Once you know the rule, you can predict the 10th number, or the 100th, without counting them all out.

---

## Visualizing Patterns

Let's see a pattern as a picture:

```
Step 1:  *
Step 2:  **
Step 3:  ***
Step 4:  ****
Step 5:  *****
```

This is the pattern 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Each step adds one star.

Here's a different pattern:

```
Step 1:  *
Step 2:  **
Step 3:  ****
Step 4:  ********
Step 5:  ****************
```

This is the doubling pattern: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16. See how fast it explodes?

Pictures help you see patterns that might be hard to spot in just numbers.
