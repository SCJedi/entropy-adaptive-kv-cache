# Lesson 7: Mathematical Rigor -- The AQFT Construction

## Overview

The cell vacuum is not merely a heuristic or a suggestive analogy. It has been rigorously constructed within algebraic quantum field theory (AQFT), the most mathematically precise formulation of quantum field theory. This lesson establishes that the cell vacuum is a legitimate state on the field algebra, satisfies the Hadamard condition required for well-defined stress-energy, is unitarily inequivalent to the standard mode vacuum, and evades the Reeh-Schlieder theorem through the same mechanism as thermal states. These results constitute the strongest mathematical foundation of the Two Vacua Theory.

A critical distinction runs through this entire lesson: AQFT is **hospitable** to the cell vacuum, not **generative**. The mathematical framework accepts the cell vacuum as a legitimate state. It does not produce it, select it, or prefer it over other states. That selection requires additional physical input beyond AQFT.

## 7.1 Algebraic Quantum Field Theory: The Framework

Standard quantum field theory begins with a Hilbert space and constructs operators on it. AQFT reverses this: it begins with an abstract algebra of observables and treats states as secondary objects -- positive linear functionals on the algebra. **[PROVEN]**

The key advantage: different states can define different (unitarily inequivalent) Hilbert space representations of the same algebra. This is impossible in ordinary quantum mechanics (where the Stone-von Neumann theorem guarantees a unique representation), but it is a fundamental feature of systems with infinitely many degrees of freedom. **[PROVEN]**

### The Weyl algebra

For a free scalar field, the relevant algebra is the Weyl algebra $\mathcal{W}$, generated by exponentiated field operators $W(f) = e^{i\phi(f)}$ for test functions $f$. The Weyl relations encode the canonical commutation relations:

$$
W(f) W(g) = e^{-i\sigma(f,g)/2} W(f + g)
$$

where $\sigma$ is the symplectic form on the space of initial data. This is a $C^*$-algebra, and its states are positive normalized linear functionals $\omega: \mathcal{W} \to \mathbb{C}$. **[PROVEN]**

### States as functionals

A state $\omega$ on $\mathcal{W}$ gives expectation values: $\omega(A)$ is "the expectation of observable $A$ in state $\omega$." The GNS (Gelfand-Naimark-Segal) construction then builds a Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}_\omega$, a representation $\pi_\omega$, and a cyclic vector $|\Omega_\omega\rangle$ such that:

$$
\omega(A) = \langle \Omega_\omega | \pi_\omega(A) | \Omega_\omega \rangle
$$

Different states can produce unitarily inequivalent GNS representations. **[PROVEN]**

## 7.2 The Cell Vacuum as an AQFT State

The cell vacuum defines a state $\omega_\Omega$ on the Weyl algebra:

$$
\omega_\Omega(A) = \langle \Omega | A | \Omega \rangle
$$

where $|\Omega\rangle = \bigotimes_n |\alpha_n\rangle$ with $|\alpha_n|^2 = 1/2$ for each Compton cell.

**Theorem:** $\omega_\Omega$ is a well-defined, positive, normalized linear functional on the Weyl algebra. **[PROVEN]** (Rigor A for finite volume, Rigor B for infinite volume.)

The finite-volume case is straightforward: a finite tensor product of coherent states is a well-defined vector in the tensor product Hilbert space. The infinite-volume case requires the machinery of infinite tensor products (von Neumann's incomplete direct product), where convergence must be established. The state converges because the displacement parameters $\alpha_n$ are uniformly bounded. **[PROVEN]**

## 7.3 Reeh-Schlieder Evasion

The Reeh-Schlieder theorem is one of the most striking results in axiomatic quantum field theory:

**Reeh-Schlieder Theorem:** Let $|\psi\rangle$ be a state satisfying the spectrum condition (energy-momentum support in the forward light cone). Then for any bounded open region $\mathcal{O}$, the set $\{A|\psi\rangle : A \in \mathcal{A}(\mathcal{O})\}$ is dense in the Hilbert space. **[PROVEN]**

This means that any state satisfying the spectrum condition is unavoidably entangled across any spatial partition. The mode vacuum $|0\rangle$ satisfies the spectrum condition, so it must be entangled across all regions -- consistent with its known long-range correlations. **[PROVEN]**

### Why it does not apply to the cell vacuum

The cell vacuum $|\Omega\rangle$ **violates the spectrum condition**. It is not Poincare-invariant: it has a definite spatial structure (Compton-scale cells), which means it does not transform properly under boosts. The energy-momentum content of $|\Omega\rangle$ is not confined to the forward light cone. **[PROVEN]**

Since the Reeh-Schlieder theorem requires the spectrum condition as a hypothesis, and the cell vacuum does not satisfy this hypothesis, the theorem does not apply. The cell vacuum is therefore free to be a product state with zero entanglement between cells, which is exactly what it is. **[PROVEN]**

### This is a feature, not a bug

The same evasion mechanism operates for thermal states (KMS states). A thermal state at temperature $T > 0$ violates the spectrum condition because thermal fluctuations populate modes outside the forward light cone. Thermal states are among the most physically important states in quantum field theory, and no one considers their spectrum condition violation pathological. The cell vacuum is in the same category. **[PROVEN]**

## 7.4 Unitary Inequivalence: The Shale-Stinespring Theorem

The mode vacuum $|0\rangle$ and the cell vacuum $|\Omega\rangle$ define unitarily inequivalent GNS representations of the Weyl algebra. No unitary operator $U$ exists such that $|\Omega\rangle = U|0\rangle$. **[PROVEN]**

### The Shale-Stinespring criterion

Two quasi-free states on the Weyl algebra of a free field are unitarily equivalent if and only if their associated Bogoliubov transformation has Hilbert-Schmidt off-diagonal blocks:

$$
\sum_{k'} |\beta_{kk'}|^2 < \infty \quad \text{(Hilbert-Schmidt condition)}
$$

where $\beta_{kk'}$ are the Bogoliubov coefficients relating the two sets of annihilation operators. **[PROVEN]**

### Application to the cell vacuum

The cell vacuum is related to the mode vacuum by a displacement (not a Bogoliubov rotation in the traditional sense, but the Shale-Stinespring criterion generalizes). The relevant quantity is the norm of the displacement:

$$
\|\alpha\|^2 = \sum_n |\alpha_n|^2 = \frac{N}{2}
$$

where $N$ is the number of Compton cells. As $N \to \infty$, this sum diverges: $\|\alpha\|^2 = N/2 \to \infty$. **[PROVEN]**

The divergence of the displacement norm means the Hilbert-Schmidt condition fails, and the two representations are unitarily inequivalent. Physically, the two states live in genuinely different Hilbert spaces -- different superselection sectors of the theory. **[PROVEN]**

### Physical meaning

Unitary inequivalence means:
- There is no finite-energy process that transforms $|0\rangle$ into $|\Omega\rangle$ or vice versa
- The two states cannot be superposed: $a|0\rangle + b|\Omega\rangle$ is not a valid state
- Physics in the two sectors is described by different (though related) Hilbert spaces
- This is a feature of infinite systems; for any finite $N$, the states are inequivalent but live in the same Hilbert space

## 7.5 The Hadamard Condition

For a state to have a well-defined renormalized stress-energy tensor $\langle T_{\mu\nu} \rangle_{\text{ren}}$, its two-point function must satisfy the Hadamard condition. This condition ensures that the short-distance (UV) singularity structure matches that of the Minkowski vacuum, so that the standard renormalization procedures apply. **[PROVEN]**

### Statement

The two-point function $W(x,y) = \langle \psi | \phi(x)\phi(y) | \psi \rangle$ satisfies the Hadamard condition if it has the form:

$$
W(x,y) = \frac{1}{4\pi^2 \sigma(x,y)} + v(x,y) \log \sigma(x,y) + \text{smooth terms}
$$

where $\sigma(x,y)$ is the squared geodesic distance (with appropriate $i\epsilon$ prescription). The leading singularity and the log term must match the standard Hadamard parametrix. **[PROVEN]**

### The cell vacuum satisfies Hadamard

The cell vacuum two-point function is:

$$
W_\Omega(x,y) = \langle \Omega | \phi(x)\phi(y) | \Omega \rangle = W_0(x,y) + F(x) \cdot F(y)
$$

where $W_0$ is the mode vacuum two-point function (which satisfies Hadamard by construction) and $F(x) = \langle \Omega | \phi(x) | \Omega \rangle$ is the field expectation value, which is a smooth function of $x$. **[PROVEN]**

The product $F(x) \cdot F(y)$ is smooth everywhere -- it contributes no singularity. Therefore the UV singularity structure of $W_\Omega$ is identical to that of $W_0$. The Hadamard condition is satisfied. **[PROVEN]**

This result is crucial: it means the renormalized stress-energy tensor $\langle \Omega | T_{\mu\nu} | \Omega \rangle_{\text{ren}}$ is well-defined using standard Hadamard point-splitting. The cell vacuum is not just a mathematical curiosity; it is a state for which all the physical machinery of semiclassical gravity applies.

## 7.6 Renormalized Stress-Energy

Given that the Hadamard condition is satisfied, the renormalized expectation value of the stress-energy tensor is defined by Hadamard point-splitting:

$$
\langle T_{\mu\nu}(x) \rangle_{\text{ren}} = \lim_{y \to x} \left[ D_{\mu\nu}(x,y) \left( W(x,y) - H(x,y) \right) \right]
$$

where $D_{\mu\nu}$ is a differential operator and $H(x,y)$ is the Hadamard parametrix (the singular part that is subtracted). **[PROVEN]**

For the cell vacuum, this procedure yields a finite, well-defined energy density. The smooth displacement $F(x) \cdot F(y)$ contributes a regular piece to $\langle T_{\mu\nu} \rangle_{\text{ren}}$ that encodes the cell vacuum's energy content. **[PROVEN]**

## 7.7 AQFT Is Hospitable, Not Generative

This distinction is essential and must not be blurred.

**What AQFT proves:**
- The cell vacuum is a legitimate state on the field algebra
- It satisfies all conditions needed for physical calculations (Hadamard, well-defined stress-energy)
- It lives in a different superselection sector from the mode vacuum (unitarily inequivalent)
- It evades the Reeh-Schlieder theorem via the same mechanism as thermal states

**What AQFT does not prove:**
- That the cell vacuum is the physically correct state for gravitational calculations
- That nature "chooses" the cell vacuum over other states
- That the cell vacuum produces the observed dark energy
- That the cosmological constant problem is resolved

AQFT provides the mathematical stage. It does not write the script. The cell vacuum is a valid character in the play, but whether it appears in nature's production is a physical question that requires experimental evidence. **[FRAMEWORK]** for the physical claims; **[PROVEN]** for the mathematical existence.

## 7.8 Curved Spacetime Extension

The AQFT framework extends naturally to curved spacetime via the locally covariant approach. On any globally hyperbolic spacetime $(\mathcal{M}, g)$, one can construct the Weyl algebra of a free scalar field and define states on it. **[PROVEN]**

The cell vacuum construction can be formulated in this framework. The key result is self-consistency: the back-reaction of the cell vacuum energy density on the spacetime geometry produces corrections of order:

$$
\frac{\delta \rho}{\rho} \sim R \lambda_C^2 \sim \frac{\rho_\Omega}{\rho_{\text{Planck}}} \sim 3.6 \times 10^{-69}
$$

where $R$ is the Ricci scalar of the background spacetime and $\lambda_C$ is the Compton wavelength. This correction is negligible for all post-nucleosynthesis cosmology. **[PROVEN]**

Parker particle creation -- the production of particles by the expanding spacetime -- is also negligible. The adiabatic parameter for the cell vacuum is:

$$
\epsilon = \frac{\dot{\omega}}{\omega^2} \sim \frac{H}{mc^2/\hbar} \sim 6.2 \times 10^{-31}
$$

where $H$ is the Hubble rate. Since $\epsilon \ll 1$, the cell oscillators evolve adiabatically and particle creation is exponentially suppressed. **[PROVEN]**
