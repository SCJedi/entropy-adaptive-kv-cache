# Primer: The Numbers -- Predictions and the 16pi^2 Factor

## The Big Idea

The cell vacuum energy density formula $\rho = m^4 c^5 / \hbar^3$ contains one free parameter: the mass $m$. Setting this equal to the observed dark energy density extracts $m \approx 2.31$ meV -- tantalizingly close to the expected scale of the lightest neutrino mass. Combined with neutrino oscillation data, this predicts a specific neutrino mass spectrum with a total mass sum around 61 meV. But there is a critical subtlety: the mass extraction is circular, and the theory's credibility hinges on independent confirmation.

## Dimensional Uniqueness

The formula $\rho = m^4 c^5/\hbar^3$ is not a guess. It is the *unique* way to build an energy density from the three quantities $m$, $c$, and $\hbar$, up to a dimensionless prefactor. The proof is a simple $3 \times 3$ linear system equating dimensions of mass, length, and time. The system has determinant $-1$ and a unique solution: the exponents $a = 4$, $b = 5$, $d = -3$. No other power-law combination works. **[PROVEN]**

The dimensionless prefactor ($K = 1$ in the cell vacuum construction) is a physical input, not determined by dimensional analysis alone. But the *scaling* -- energy density proportional to $m^4$ -- is fixed by mathematics.

## The 16pi^2 Factor

In the standard mode vacuum calculation, the energy density involves the factor $16\pi^2$ in the denominator: $\rho_{\text{mode}} \sim \Lambda^4/(16\pi^2)$. This was once claimed as a fundamental constant connecting the two vacua.

It is not. The factor $16\pi^2$ is the solid-angle factor from integrating over three-dimensional momentum space. In $d$ spatial dimensions, the analogous factor changes: it is $4\pi$ in one dimension, $12\pi$ in two, and $16\pi^2$ in three. It depends on the number of spatial dimensions, on the dispersion relation, and on the cutoff convention. For massive fields in three dimensions, the factor drops from $16\pi^2 \approx 158$ to approximately 103, a correction of about 1.53 from the dispersion relation.

This demotion does not affect the cell vacuum construction itself -- it only removes a previously claimed interpretive layer. **[DEMOTED]**

## The Circularity Problem

Here is the most important intellectual point of this lesson. The "prediction" that cell vacuum energy matches observed dark energy is circular:

1. Observe $\rho_\Lambda$ (dark energy density)
2. Assume $\rho_\Lambda = m^4 c^5/\hbar^3$
3. Solve for $m$: get $m_1 \approx 2.31$ meV
4. Compute $\rho_{\text{cell}} = m_1^4 c^5/\hbar^3$ -- of course it equals $\rho_\Lambda$; that is how $m_1$ was defined

The "0.4% match" previously advertised as evidence has been **retired**. It is guaranteed by construction, not derived from independent data. The prediction becomes non-trivial only when $m_1$ is independently measured -- for example, through cosmological neutrino mass constraints or direct measurement experiments.

## The Neutrino Mass Spectrum

The genuinely testable content emerges when the extracted $m_1$ is combined with independently measured oscillation parameters. Neutrino oscillation experiments measure the squared mass differences $\Delta m_{21}^2$ and $\Delta m_{31}^2$ with high precision. Using these:

- $m_1 \approx 2.31$ meV (from dark energy density)
- $m_2 \approx 9.0$ meV (from $m_1$ and $\Delta m_{21}^2$)
- $m_3 \approx 49.6$ meV (from $m_1$ and $\Delta m_{31}^2$)
- $\Sigma m_\nu \approx 60.9$ meV

This total mass sum is testable by cosmological surveys. Current bounds from DESI DR2 give $\Sigma < 53$ meV at 95% confidence, creating 1.5--2$\sigma$ tension with the prediction. Not fatal, but the theory is under experimental pressure. **[TENSION]**

The fourth-root in the mass extraction ($m \propto \rho^{1/4}$) provides a useful shield: a 10--15% uncertainty in the dark energy density propagates to only 2.5--3.5% uncertainty in $m_1$.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Dimensional uniqueness is real.** The $m^4 c^5/\hbar^3$ formula is the only game in town for power-law energy density from $m$, $c$, $\hbar$. The exponents are mathematically unique.

2. **The density match is circular.** Extracting $m_1$ from $\rho_\Lambda$ and then celebrating that $m_1^4 c^5/\hbar^3 = \rho_\Lambda$ is tautological. This claim has been retired.

3. **The neutrino spectrum is genuinely predictive.** Combining the extracted $m_1$ with oscillation data gives $\Sigma \approx 61$ meV, testable within a decade by DESI, CMB-S4, and direct experiments.

4. **16pi^2 is geometry, not physics.** It depends on spatial dimension and dispersion relation. The massive field value is approximately 103, not 158.

5. **The theory is under pressure.** DESI DR2 creates real tension. The framework has zero free parameters to adjust. If the prediction fails, it fails cleanly.
