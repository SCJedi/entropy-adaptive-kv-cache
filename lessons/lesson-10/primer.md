# Primer: Open Problems and the Road Ahead

## Where Things Stand

The Two Vacua Theory has been through the fire. The AQFT mathematical construction is proven and solid. The neutrino mass predictions are sharp and testable. But the central physical claim -- that the cell vacuum explains dark energy -- has been abandoned after two independent teams proved the equation of state is $w = 0$ (cold dark matter), not $w = -1$ (cosmological constant).

This primer maps what remains: the open problems, the experimental tests, and the paths that might lead forward.

## The Black Hole Problem

The most serious unresolved tension is with black hole entropy. The Bekenstein-Hawking formula $S = A/(4l_P^2)$ gives every black hole an enormous entropy -- about $10^{77}$ bits for a solar-mass black hole. A leading explanation is that this entropy comes from entanglement of the vacuum across the horizon. The mode vacuum naturally has this entanglement.

The cell vacuum has zero entanglement. It is a product state -- no correlations between cells. If the cell vacuum is the correct description for gravity, then the entanglement entropy across ANY surface is zero. Including black hole horizons.

This is a direct conflict. Possible ways out include: the vacuum might be scale-dependent (cell-like at cosmological scales, mode-like near black holes), black hole formation might dynamically generate entanglement, or Bekenstein-Hawking entropy might not be entanglement entropy at all. All of these are speculative. None are formalized. This problem is existential for the framework.

## The Mass Selection Gap

The framework says the lightest neutrino mass determines the vacuum energy. But there is no mechanism explaining WHY only the lightest neutrino matters. Why not the electron? Why not all neutrinos combined? This is the biggest conceptual gap -- a free assumption, not a derivation.

## The DESI Tension

The framework predicts $\Sigma m_\nu \approx 61$ meV. DESI Data Release 2 sets an upper bound of $\Sigma < 53$ meV at 95% confidence. That is a 1.5-2 sigma tension -- not enough to kill the prediction (physics requires 3-5 sigma), but not comfortable either.

Importantly, this tension affects ALL normal-ordering scenarios with non-negligible lightest neutrino mass, not just this framework. The oscillation data require $\Sigma > 58$ meV for normal ordering with $m_1 > 0$. If the bounds tighten further, all such scenarios are in trouble.

## What Was Demoted

Several claims that seemed to provide deep mathematical structure have been shown to be limited or incorrect:

- **Fenchel duality** between the two vacua: failed as a theorem (the cell vacuum energy is a number, not a function)
- **$16\pi^2$ as a fundamental constant:** it depends on spatial dimension and is purely geometric
- **Variational uniqueness** of $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$: it is algebraic, not variational; the critical point is a maximum
- **$10^{123}$ as a duality gap:** no optimization structure exists
- **Modular theory and category theory connections:** no content at the current stage
- **Cell vacuum as dark energy:** killed by $w = 0$

## The Experimental Roadmap

The framework makes zero-free-parameter predictions. Experiments will decide.

**Near-term (2025-2028):** DESI DR3+ will tighten the neutrino mass bound to $\sim 40$ meV sensitivity. JUNO will determine the mass ordering at $>3\sigma$. Euclid will provide complementary cosmological constraints.

**Medium-term (2030s):** CMB-S4 is the definitive test -- sensitivity of $\sim 15$-$20$ meV on $\Sigma m_\nu$. DUNE will determine mass ordering at $5\sigma$.

**Kill shots:** $\Sigma < 45$ meV at $>3\sigma$ kills the framework. Inverted ordering at $>5\sigma$ kills it. Detection of $\Sigma \approx 60$-$62$ meV would confirm the mass prediction.

## Possible Paths Forward

**Dark matter reinterpretation:** Since $w = 0$, the cell vacuum is cold dark matter. Its energy density ($\sim 2.5\times$ observed dark matter density) is at least in the right ballpark. Whether this can be made consistent with observations is an open question.

**Alternative constructions:** The no-go result (finiteness vs. $w = -1$) applies to massive scalar fields with cell structure. Other approaches might avoid the tension -- but none have been found.

**The category error survives:** Even if the cell vacuum is not dark energy, the observation that the mode vacuum is the wrong state for local gravitational questions may point toward the correct resolution. The diagnosis might outlive the prescription.

## The Bottom Line

The Two Vacua Theory is a genuine theoretical contribution with significant implementation gaps. The mathematics is proven. The predictions are sharp. The central physical interpretation has been honestly abandoned after rigorous self-testing.

The framework's probability as a dark energy theory is 5-10%. Its probability of containing correct insights is higher. The neutrino mass predictions are interpretation-independent and will be tested definitively within a decade.

No free parameters. No place to hide. Nature will decide.
