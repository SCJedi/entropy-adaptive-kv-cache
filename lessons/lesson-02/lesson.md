# Lesson 2: Coherent States -- Nature's Preferred Quantum States

## Overview

Coherent states are the quantum states that most closely resemble classical oscillations. They are eigenstates of the annihilation operator, saturate the uncertainty principle, and form an overcomplete basis for the Hilbert space. The special case $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ plays a central role in later lessons: it carries exactly one quantum of rest energy $mc^2$ per oscillator, and it is a fixed point of every natural duality transformation connecting conjugate descriptions of the oscillator.

All results in Sections 2.1--2.7 are **[PROVEN]** (standard quantum optics). Section 2.8 on self-duality is **[PROVEN]** as mathematics; its physical significance for vacuum energy is **[FRAMEWORK]**.

## 2.1 Definition: Eigenstates of the Annihilation Operator

A coherent state $|\alpha\rangle$ is defined as an eigenstate of the annihilation operator:

$$
\hat{a}|\alpha\rangle = \alpha|\alpha\rangle
$$

where $\alpha$ is a complex number. Since $\hat{a}$ is not Hermitian, its eigenvalues need not be real, and in fact $\alpha$ can be any element of $\mathbb{C}$. **[PROVEN]**

This definition is deceptively simple. It says: the coherent state is the state from which you cannot extract information about a definite particle by removing one quantum. The annihilation operator acts, and the state simply rescales. No collapse to a lower-energy eigenstate. The state "replenishes" what was taken.

## 2.2 Expansion in Number States

To construct $|\alpha\rangle$ explicitly, expand in the number basis:

$$
|\alpha\rangle = e^{-|\alpha|^2/2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{\alpha^n}{\sqrt{n!}}\,|n\rangle
$$

**Proof:** Apply $\hat{a}$ to this expansion. Using $\hat{a}|n\rangle = \sqrt{n}\,|n-1\rangle$:

$$
\hat{a}|\alpha\rangle = e^{-|\alpha|^2/2} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\alpha^n}{\sqrt{n!}}\sqrt{n}\,|n-1\rangle = e^{-|\alpha|^2/2} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\alpha^n}{\sqrt{(n-1)!}}\,|n-1\rangle
$$

Re-indexing with $m = n-1$:

$$
= \alpha\, e^{-|\alpha|^2/2} \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \frac{\alpha^m}{\sqrt{m!}}\,|m\rangle = \alpha\,|\alpha\rangle
$$

The prefactor $e^{-|\alpha|^2/2}$ ensures normalization: $\langle\alpha|\alpha\rangle = 1$. **[PROVEN]**

## 2.3 Physical Interpretation

The coherent state has a vivid physical interpretation in the position representation. Its wavefunction is:

$$
\psi_\alpha(x) = \left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^{1/4} \exp\left(-\frac{m\omega}{2\hbar}(x - \bar{x})^2 + i\bar{p}x/\hbar\right)
$$

where $\bar{x} = \sqrt{2\hbar/(m\omega)}\,\text{Re}(\alpha)$ and $\bar{p} = \sqrt{2m\hbar\omega}\,\text{Im}(\alpha)$ are the mean position and momentum. This is a Gaussian wavepacket -- the same shape as the ground state -- centered at $(\bar{x}, \bar{p})$ in phase space rather than at the origin. The width is independent of $\alpha$: displacement moves the center without distorting the shape. **[PROVEN]**

The complex parameter $\alpha$ encodes both the position and momentum of the oscillator's center of motion. Its modulus $|\alpha|$ determines the amplitude of oscillation, and its phase $\arg(\alpha)$ determines where in the oscillation cycle the state is at $t = 0$. As time evolves, $\alpha$ rotates in the complex plane, and the wavepacket follows a circular trajectory in phase space -- exactly mirroring the classical oscillator. **[PROVEN]**

## 2.4 The Displacement Operator (Alternative Construction)

Coherent states can also be generated by displacing the vacuum:

$$
|\alpha\rangle = \hat{D}(\alpha)|0\rangle
$$

where the displacement operator is:

$$
\hat{D}(\alpha) = \exp\left(\alpha\hat{a}^\dagger - \alpha^*\hat{a}\right)
$$

This is a unitary operator: $\hat{D}^\dagger(\alpha) = \hat{D}(-\alpha) = \hat{D}^{-1}(\alpha)$. Physically, it shifts the center of the ground-state Gaussian wavepacket to a new point in phase space determined by $\alpha$. **[PROVEN]**

## 2.5 Poisson Statistics

The probability of finding $n$ particles in a coherent state is:

$$
P(n) = |\langle n|\alpha\rangle|^2 = e^{-|\alpha|^2}\frac{|\alpha|^{2n}}{n!}
$$

This is a Poisson distribution with mean $\langle \hat{n}\rangle = |\alpha|^2$ and variance $\Delta n^2 = |\alpha|^2$. **[PROVEN]**

Key consequence: the particle number in a coherent state is uncertain. The relative fluctuation is $\Delta n / \langle n\rangle = 1/\sqrt{|\alpha|^2}$, which decreases for large $|\alpha|^2$ (approaching classical behavior) but is always nonzero.

## 2.6 Minimum Uncertainty

The coherent state saturates the Heisenberg bound:

$$
\Delta x = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2m\omega}}, \qquad \Delta p = \sqrt{\frac{m\hbar\omega}{2}}
$$

$$
\Delta x \cdot \Delta p = \frac{\hbar}{2}
$$

These are the same uncertainties as the ground state $|0\rangle$. What changes is the center of the wavepacket, not its width. The coherent state is a displaced ground state -- same shape, different location in phase space. **[PROVEN]**

This is why coherent states are the "most classical" quantum states: they achieve the minimum quantum uncertainty while having nonzero expectation values for both $\hat{x}$ and $\hat{p}$ (unlike number states).

## 2.7 Energy of a Coherent State

The expectation value of the Hamiltonian in a coherent state is:

$$
\langle\alpha|H|\alpha\rangle = \hbar\omega\left(|\alpha|^2 + \frac{1}{2}\right)
$$

**Proof:** $\langle H\rangle = \hbar\omega(\langle\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a}\rangle + 1/2) = \hbar\omega(|\alpha|^2 + 1/2)$, since $\langle\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a}\rangle = |\alpha|^2$ for a coherent state. **[PROVEN]**

The energy splits into two contributions:
- **Zero-point energy:** $\hbar\omega/2$ (the same as the ground state)
- **Coherent excitation energy:** $\hbar\omega|\alpha|^2$ (from the displacement)

## 2.8 Overcompleteness

The coherent states form an overcomplete basis:

$$
\frac{1}{\pi}\int |\alpha\rangle\langle\alpha|\,d^2\alpha = \hat{I}
$$

where the integral is over the entire complex plane. This is a resolution of the identity, so any state can be expanded in terms of coherent states. However, the expansion is not unique -- the basis is overcomplete. **[PROVEN]**

Coherent states are not orthogonal:

$$
|\langle\alpha|\beta\rangle|^2 = e^{-|\alpha - \beta|^2}
$$

The overlap is exponentially small for well-separated states but never exactly zero. **[PROVEN]**

## 2.9 The Special Case: $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$

When $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$, several remarkable properties converge.

### Energy: Exactly One Quantum

$$
\langle H \rangle = \hbar\omega\left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\right) = \hbar\omega
$$

The coherent excitation energy equals the zero-point energy, and the total is exactly one quantum $\hbar\omega$. If we identify $\omega = mc^2/\hbar$ (the Compton frequency of a particle of mass $m$), this is exactly the rest energy $mc^2$. **[PROVEN]**

### Particle Number Statistics

$$
P(0) = e^{-1/2} \approx 0.607, \qquad P(1) = \tfrac{1}{2}e^{-1/2} \approx 0.303
$$

$$
\langle n \rangle = \frac{1}{2}, \qquad \Delta n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}
$$

A state with energy $\hbar\omega$ -- "one quantum" -- has a 60.7% probability of containing zero particles and a 30.3% probability of containing one particle. The mean particle number is $1/2$. **[PROVEN]**

This is a vivid illustration of the distinction between energy and particle number in quantum mechanics.

### Energy Equipartition

The kinetic and potential energy contributions are:

$$
\langle T \rangle = \frac{\langle p^2\rangle}{2m} = \frac{\hbar\omega}{2}, \qquad \langle V \rangle = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2\langle x^2\rangle = \frac{\hbar\omega}{2}
$$

Perfect equipartition: $\langle T \rangle = \langle V \rangle = \hbar\omega/2$. This is the only value of $|\alpha|^2$ for which the coherent excitation is evenly split between kinetic and potential energy on top of the ground state. **[PROVEN]**

### The Self-Duality Theorem

The value $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ corresponds to the unique self-dual point under three interconnected transformations:

1. **Legendre self-duality:** The function $f(x) = x^2/2$ is its own Fenchel-Legendre conjugate: $f^*(p) = p^2/2$. This is the unique convex function with this property. The quadratic potential of the harmonic oscillator at $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ sits at this self-dual point. **[PROVEN]**

2. **Fourier self-duality:** The Gaussian $e^{-x^2/2}$ is a fixed point of the Fourier transform (up to normalization). It is the unique Schwartz function with this property. The ground-state wavefunction of the harmonic oscillator is precisely this Gaussian. **[PROVEN]**

3. **Energy equipartition:** As shown above, $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ is the unique value at which kinetic and potential energies are equal. **[PROVEN]**

These three self-dualities are mathematically connected: Legendre self-duality of $f$ implies Fourier self-duality of $e^{-f}$, and both imply energy equipartition at the corresponding coherent state parameter. **[PROVEN]**

### Algebraic, Not Variational

The value $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ is determined algebraically: if each oscillator carries energy $\hbar\omega = mc^2$, then $\hbar\omega(|\alpha|^2 + 1/2) = \hbar\omega$ forces $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$. This is a simple equation, not the result of minimizing or maximizing a functional. **[PROVEN]**

An earlier claim that $|\alpha|^2 = 1/2$ is variationally unique (minimizing some physically meaningful functional) has been **[DEMOTED]**. The variational argument selects coherent states over squeezed states at fixed energy, but when the mass is treated as a free parameter, the critical point of the variance is a maximum, not a minimum. The algebraic determination stands; the variational gloss does not.

## 2.10 Time Evolution

A coherent state remains coherent under time evolution:

$$
|\alpha(t)\rangle = |e^{-i\omega t}\alpha\rangle
$$

The parameter $\alpha$ rotates in the complex plane at frequency $\omega$. The center of the wavepacket traces a circle in phase space -- exactly the classical trajectory. This is another sense in which coherent states are "most classical." **[PROVEN]**

Compare with a number state: $|n(t)\rangle = e^{-iE_n t/\hbar}|n\rangle$. The number state acquires a global phase but its physical properties (position and momentum distributions) do not change. It does not oscillate.
